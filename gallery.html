Uzbek Craft Exhibition

First time ever traditional handmade craft exhibition in Massachusetts, dedicated to the national holiday Navruz. Hand-embroidered bed covers, tapestries, pillow-cases, gold-embroidered gowns, head wears, cases for glasses, national “Pakhtagul” dishes will be on display, together with national music and dance. Traditional pastries will be served. You will enjoy our custom of henna-making, kids activities and much more.

Time and date: March 23, 2018, 11 am – 3 pm
Location: Watertown Free Library
123 Main St, Watertown, MA 02472

Heritage Craft Fair 2018

Saturday, March 24, 2018

Every year Heritage Fair will offer a wonderful variety of hand-crafted items, featuring such treats as glassware, clothing, toys, jewelry, woodworking, leather goods, quilts, metalwork, paintings, photography, delicious fudge, jams, and specialty oils and vinegars. Each year there's a more impressive variety of exceptional items than ever before. Admission to the displays of more than 100 crafters is designed to be affordable for visitors to the Fair, while also providing support for the mission of the Heritage Chorale, whose members are committed to enriching the lives of singers and audiences in the Metrowest area.

For more information, please go to heritagechorale.org

Time and date: March 24, 2018, 10 am – 3 pm
Location: Keefe Tech High School 750 Winter Street
Framingham, MA

Exhibition in Newton Free Library

First time ever display exhibition in Newton Free Library in Massachusetts, dedicated to Uzbekistan and its culture. Every display depicts the traditional craft of Uzbekistan with its explanation for the local people: handmade and machine-made silk embroidery and gold-embroidered items as well as traditional Pakhtagul dishes.

Time and date: June 6, 2018, 11 am – 2:30 pm
Location: 330 Homer St, Newton, MA 02459

Cultural Survival Bazaar

Cultural Survival Bazaars are a series of cultural festivals that provide Indigenous artists, cooperatives, and their representatives from around the world the chance to sell their work directly to the US public. Each event features traditional and contemporary crafts, artwork, clothing, jewelry, home goods, and accessories from dozens of countries.

Time and date: July 21 and 22, 2018, 10 am – 5 pm
Location: Newburyport, MA, USA
